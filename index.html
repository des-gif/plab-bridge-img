Example · Single best answer PLAB 1 style

A 24-year-old presents with a sudden, severe headache that reached maximum intensity within seconds — "the worst of my life." Neurological examination is normal. What is the most appropriate first-line investigation?

Correct: B — Non-contrast CT head. A thunderclap headache raises suspicion of subarachnoid haemorrhage; an urgent non-contrast CT is the first-line investigation. A lumbar puncture is considered only if the CT is negative and suspicion remains.

UK practice note: follow NICE and your local pathway for thunderclap headache — early imaging and timely senior escalation matter.

Why it exists

You're already a doctor. This is about a different system.

Many International Medical Graduates don't struggle with medicine — they struggle with applying it the way UK practice expects. PLAB 1 tests safe, guideline-based, NHS-style reasoning, not just recall.

PLAB Bridge IMG makes that shift clearer and less overwhelming: structured revision, exam-style practice, and explanations grounded in how UK clinicians prioritise patient safety and decide what to do next.
